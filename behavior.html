CAT PSYCHOLOGY

Cats, with their supple movements and sharp eyes, captivate us with distinctive vocalizations, offering a glimpse into their complex world.

Communication: Cats employ nuanced methods like tail flicks, focused gazes, or purrs, conveying a range of emotions and intentions.

Body Language: Silent yet expressive, a simple head tilt or back arch speaks volumes.

BEHAVIOR'S CAT PROBLEM

Fear aggression occurs in situations that make a cat afraid. Fear causes many types of aggression

.

Fearful cats may try to avoid the triggering threat but can become aggressive when they cannot escape or learn that acting aggressively can remove the threat

.

Animals that learn that aggression "works" to eliminate threats may act aggressively even when they are not threatened.
Poor socialization, temperament (inherited from parents), previous punishment, and learning can also lead to fear aggression.
